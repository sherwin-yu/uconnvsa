Union of North American Vietnamese Student Associations (UNAVSA)

The Union of North American Vietnamese Student Associations (UNAVSA) is a 501(c)(3) non-profit, non-partisan, community-based organization. UNAVSA is dedicated to cultivating the next generation of leaders who will serve as stewards of the Vietnamese community. Officially formed in 2004, student leaders from Southern California and New England recognized an unmet need within the Vietnamese youth community focused on the development of the next generation of leaders. From this collaboration, the Union of North American Vietnamese Student Associations (UNAVSA) was created to develop and organize a large, supportive network throughout the United States and Canada. The first conference was held in Boston, Massachusetts in 2004, where over 200 young activists from across the country and from different community-based organizations came together to develop the foundation for what UNAVSA has accomplished to date. With the first election of eleven regional representatives and five executive officers, the delegates outlined the structure that has evolved into the Council of Regional Representatives, the Executive Board, and the President’s Council.

Regional Level

New England Intercollegiate Vietnamese Student Association (NEIVSA)

Founded in 1985, NEIVSA strives to unify the Vietnamese American Community of New England.

In NEIVSA, we strive to unify the Vietnamese American Community of New England by encouraging participation, leadership, and service; and maintaining a network that will serve as a valuable resource for the community and students. Tong Hoi organizes social, cultural, and educational events that seek to: *Foster awareness of Vietnamese/Vietnamese American culture, language, history on college campuses and throughout New England. *Promote the interests of the Vietnamese/Vietnamese American student bodies. *Maintain a network that will serve as a valuable resource for Vietnamese/Vietnamese American students. *Encourage participation, leadership and service among Vietnamese/Vietnamese American students.
